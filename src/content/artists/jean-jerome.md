						<h2><strong>ABF CLUB // Every Wednesday 22h CET (= 21h UTC)</strong></h2><p>&nbsp;</p><p><strong>Jean-Jérôme</strong> (often stylized as <strong>Jean Jerome</strong> or <strong>Jean-Jerome</strong>) is a <strong>French DJ, producer</strong>, and <strong>sound designer</strong>, a respected figure in the Paris house music scene. A specialist of <strong>original house music</strong>, heavily influenced by <strong>Detroit techno</strong> and <strong>Chicago/New York house</strong>, he left his mark on the late 90s and 2000s through his groovy productions and energetic sets.</p><h3>Beginnings in electronic music</h3><p>Passionate about electronic music from the early 1990s, Jean-Jérôme discovered the rave and techno scenes by regularly attending iconic Paris parties (such as Cosmos Factory at Mozinor). Initially a <strong>hardcore</strong> fan, he started mixing unofficially in <strong>free parties</strong> as early as <strong>December 1993</strong>.</p><p>In <strong>1995</strong>, he moved away from hardcore to fully embrace <strong>house music</strong> and began performing in clubs. He quickly developed a broad yet authentic style: groovy, sunny, soulful, with a timeless club feeling that established him as a true representative of “old school” house.</p><h3>Production career &amp; the Purpleheadz project</h3><p>By the late 1990s, Jean-Jérôme entered the production world. His first house maxi was released in <strong>1998</strong> under the alias <strong>Dog's Biscuits</strong> on <strong>KIF Records</strong> (in collaboration with Gil Chabert/Klubfilter), selling over 5,000 copies.</p><p>He went on to release numerous tracks and remixes under various pseudonyms:</p><ul><li><strong>Kut'n'Paste</strong></li><li><strong>Itsic</strong></li><li><strong>The Soulbrozerz</strong></li><li><strong>Dog's Biscuits</strong></li></ul><p>In <strong>2001</strong>, together with his friend <strong>Cedric C.</strong>, he launched the <strong>Purpleheadz</strong> project, which became one of the most significant chapters of his career. The duo produced powerful, groovy house tracks, including the famous <strong>"Ramble"</strong> (2002), which turned into a <strong>genuine anthem</strong> in Parisian clubs and on European dancefloors during that era.</p><p>Notable productions and remixes include:</p><ul><li><strong>Antonio De Jesus – "Electric Lady"</strong> (Purpleheadz Remix, Illmatic 2001)</li><li><strong>Ghetto Orchestra – "Flying Toad"</strong> (Purpleheadz Rework, Illmatic Records 2002)</li><li><strong>Laurent Wolf – "Together"</strong> (Jean Jerome Remix, 2003)</li><li><strong>Aida Khann – "Music"</strong> (Jean Jerome Remix, 2003)</li><li>Collaborations with Cesar Del Rio &amp; Juan Magan, Bustafunk, and others</li></ul><p>His releases appeared on prestigious labels such as: <strong>KIF</strong>, <strong>Serial</strong>, <strong>Cyber</strong>, <strong>Instinctive</strong>, <strong>Joy Productions</strong>, <strong>Karmic Power Records</strong>, <strong>Pumpz</strong>, <strong>Iconyc</strong>, <strong>S&amp;S Records</strong>, <strong>Silent Koalas</strong>, <strong>Glamour Punk</strong>, <strong>Soul n Pepa</strong>, <strong>Deetrax</strong>, <strong>Sony Japan</strong>, and more.</p><h3>Style &amp; legacy</h3><p>Jean-Jérôme cultivates an authentic house sound: deep, groovy, with soul influences and a strong club feeling. His productions and remixes remain appreciated for their positive energy and deep respect for the roots of house music.</p><p>Still active in 2026, he continues releasing tracks and sharing mixes on platforms such as <strong>Beatport</strong>, <strong>Traxsource</strong>, and <strong>SoundCloud</strong>, maintaining a loyal following.</p><p><strong>Quote often associated with his vibe:</strong> « House Music from the origins, forever. »</p><p>A discreet yet essential veteran of the French house scene, Jean-Jérôme helped keep the authentic sound of the 90s and 2000s alive through <strong>Purpleheadz</strong> and his numerous productions. A true pillar of the Parisian electronic underground.</p>
